About Me:

I am currently a PhD candidate in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at the University of Bath, supervised by Alexander Cox (Bath) and Sigrid Källblad (KTH). I obtained my MSc in Financial Mathematics with distinction from University College London (UCL). Prior to that, I completed a BSc in Honors Mathematics at New York University Shanghai (NYUSH). From 2018 to 2020, I worked as a full-time volunteer teacher at a primary school in a rural mountainous village in Yunnan, China.

Research Interests:

My research interests lie in stochastic analysis and optimal control theory. More specifically, I focus on controlled measure-valued processes and controlled filtering problems. I am also interested in numerical methods for stochastic optimal control problems, particularly machine-learning-based approaches. More recently, I have become interested in probabilistic AI and reinforcement learning. I am also fascinated by rough path theory and stochastic analysis/optimisation/numerical methods on manifolds.
